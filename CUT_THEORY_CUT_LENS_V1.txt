THE HANDLES

These are compressed names for forced consequences.
Not extra metaphysics. Readable shortcuts.

IDENTITY
A pattern that can be recognised as the same across transitions.
Not a substance. Not an object. A criterion: "this persists enough to count as itself."
Plan G equivalent: AND-lock coherence. Committed structure that survives the cutting front.

CUT
An operation that introduces categorical separation: inside/outside.
Not assumed spatial. Space emerges later as stable adjacency.
Plan G equivalent: OR at 1D. The first direction. The first distinction.

BOUNDARY
A stabilised distinction. The persistent edge of a cut.
Primarily categorical — crossing it changes what the system treats as possible.
Plan G equivalent: IFF closure at 2D. First genuine inside/outside.

TENSION
The relational pressure created because the two sides of a boundary
are no longer the same. Not a force — a demand that difference
must now be accounted for.
Plan G equivalent: AND-lock cost differential. The pressure to reduce
cost by forming bonds or maintaining coherence.

RECURRENCE
The survival of a boundary-pattern across transitions.
Identity in action.
Plan G equivalent: forced by the null point's logic.
Having deviated from Nothing, return is unavailable.
The spiral not the loop. Always forward.

FABRIC
A network of mutually reinforcing recurrences.
Relational structure: stable continuations constrain each other.
Plan G equivalent: AND-lock network. Chemistry, biology, physics
are all fabrics at different scales.

SLICE
An operation that rewrites an existing boundary.
Cuts introduce structure. Slices transform structure.
Slices act only on what is already formed.
Plan G equivalent: update event at the cutting front.
The advance of committed structure into FoF.

DIMENSION
A recurrence-pattern that remains coherent under slice events.
An invariant degree of freedom under permitted transformations.
Plan G equivalent: dimensional threshold in the ladder.
Each dimension is a new logical operation that becomes available.

AGENCY
Active identity-preservation through selective recurrence.
Selecting continuations that maintain coherence under constraints.
Plan G equivalent: Mind threshold. The observer's cutting front
with a self-model rich enough to select coherence-preserving continuations.

THE NECESSITY CHAIN

If Identity exists and Cut is possible, the following chain is unavoidable.

1. CUT → BOUNDARY
   Inside/outside implies a boundary relation.
   Plan G: OR at 1D → IFF at 2D. The cut forces a closure.

2. BOUNDARY → TENSION
   Difference across a boundary creates relational pressure.
   Plan G: AND-lock cost differential. Structure wants to minimise cost.

3. TENSION → RECURRENCE
   Repeated resolution of tension stabilises a pattern.
   Plan G: null point recurrence. The spiral not the loop.

4. RECURRENCE → FABRIC
   Multiple stable recurrences constrain each other into a network.
   Plan G: AND-lock network building up through dimensional thresholds.

5. FABRIC → SLICE
   Once a network exists, boundary rewrites become the minimal
   form of change that preserves partial structure.
   Plan G: cutting front advancing. FoF ahead always open.

6. SLICE → DIMENSION
   Consistent slice biases stabilise invariant degrees of freedom.
   Plan G: dimensional ladder. Each threshold produces new logical operations.

7. DIMENSION → IDENTITY
   Identity becomes navigable continuity across slice sequences.
   Plan G: observer as cutting front threading coherent slices.

8. IDENTITY → AGENCY
   Once continuity exists, selection can preserve it.
   Plan G: Mind threshold. Self-modelling observer selects coherent continuations.

One-line summary:
A coherent world is identity surviving cuts, updating through slices,
stabilising into fabrics, and growing dimensions where invariants persist.

EMERGENCE
When a new stable recurrence survives slicing and becomes
a new layer of structure.
Plan G: threshold crossing in the dimensional ladder.
Chemistry from physics. Biology from chemistry. Mind from biology.
Each emergence is a new AND-lock structure that was not present below.

SCALE IN CUT LANGUAGE

Scale is boundary economics.
Resize an identity, you resize the bottleneck.
The "laws" shift because the cheapest stable recurrence shifts.

If you double a linear size L:
Surface area scales like L² — boundary grows "square."
Volume scales like L³ — inside grows "cube."
Boundary per inside goes like 1/L.

Small things: huge boundary per inside.
Fast exchange. Easy cooling. Easy diffusion.

Big things: tiny boundary per inside.
Interface bottleneck. Overheating. Transport limits.

Scale forces architecture:
More boundary (folding, branching, fractals).
Internal transport networks.
New layers of structure (organs, vasculature, hierarchy).

Why cells are small: diffusion + boundary exchange dominate.
Why lungs are folded: manufacturing boundary area to match volume demand.
Why animals need circulatory systems: boundary bottleneck solved by internal transport.
Why large systems become hierarchical: coordination tension rises.
